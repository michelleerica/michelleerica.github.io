//coding and me

I was curious about coding and wanted to be able to do it first hand, so during a one year sabbatical, I enrolled in General Assembly's Web Development Immersive. I discovered what was missing from my life: a community of support and inclusiveness, full of out of the box thinkers who want to know more .

When the course ended, I went on a two-month solo trip around the USA where I pushed myself to the physical and emotional limits in the name of self-discovery.

And now I'm back and working as a teaching assistant for the same course I graduated from just before the USA trip. Not only am I solidifying my existing skills and knowledge but I'm learning even more about communicating my thought processes when problem solving and seeing things from different perspectives.
